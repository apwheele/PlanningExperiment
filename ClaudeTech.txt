❯ Why are the 0.5's in the IRR estimator?                                       

● The +0.5 terms are a continuity correction (sometimes called a
  Haldane-Anscombe correction). They prevent the variance from blowing up to
  infinity when any of the cell counts is zero — which can happen in
  small-sample or short-duration monitoring. Without them, a single zero-count
  cell would make the standard error undefined.

  Wilson (2022) uses this form specifically because the IRR estimator is meant
  for small-count settings typical in place-based crime evaluations, where you
  might have a control area with only a handful of crimes in the early time
  periods. The +0.5 stabilizes the log-IRR variance estimate and keeps
  confidence intervals well-behaved even when counts are very low.
